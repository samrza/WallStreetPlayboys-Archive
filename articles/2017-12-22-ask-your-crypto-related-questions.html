  10. Ben says

    December 22, 2017 at 12:14 pm

    Do you really think that fiat will eventually die as a result of crypto? How soon?

    Reply

    • JonnyNova says

      December 23, 2017 at 2:16 pm

      Definitely not. Adopting something like cryptocurrencies for a national currency is seriously bad policy. The main problem with using decentralized currency as a national currency is that it overexposes your money supply to foreign threats. The volatility the cryptos have would also be terrible for both consumers and business owners.

      I’d wager in the future we will see things like better digital currencies issued by governments instead of cryptos as they exist today. Cryptos are only using a proof-of-work and a blockchain because that is the only way that trust can be decentralized. Gov issued digital currencies would dispense with mining and the blockchain and just use proof-of-authority from the central bank. Gov issued digital currencies can also have better accounting of transactions and can significantly change how taxation works.

      For a sneak peak watch Estonia’s launch of estcoin to see what kind of changes to expect from them.

      Reply
